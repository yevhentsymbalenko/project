Research Project

The Impact of Russian Propaganda on Shaping Narratives Among Ukrainian Refugees in Germany

YT

Yevhen Tsymbalenko

Researcher

Research Objectives

The project aims to investigate the mechanisms through which Russian propaganda influences the formation of narratives among Ukrainian refugees in Germany. The project seeks to understand how displaced individuals interpret, perceive, and potentially internalise media messages in the context of forced migration and adaptation to a new sociocultural environment.

1

Identifying Themes, Symbols, and Linguistic Constructs

Identify the key themes, symbols, and linguistic constructs that characterise the perception of propaganda-related media content among Ukrainian refugees in Germany. Special attention will be given to disinformation narratives that may reduce solidarity, incite intragroup conflict, and distort understanding of the sociopolitical context.

2

Analysing Propagandistic Messages Perception

Analyse how Ukrainian refugees perceive propagandistic messages, the degree to which these messages are accepted and internalised, and the impact of such narratives on identity, social behaviour, emotional responses, and perceptions of Ukraine, the European Union, and the host society.

3

Developing Recommendations

Provide empirically grounded conclusions that can inform recommendations for governmental institutions, civil society organisations, and media literacy initiatives, to enhance resilience to disinformation and strengthen democratic values within migrant communities.

Stages & Timeline

Project Stages (24 months)

Step 1 of 4 Months 1–8

WP1:
Preliminary Content Analysis of Telegram Channels

Objective: To identify key themes, symbols, and linguistic constructs in Telegram content targeted at Ukrainian refugees residing in Germany.

Key activities
  • Selection and classification of Telegram channels across different federal states.
  • Downloading and archiving of message histories from the selected channels over the past year.
  • Identification of keywords, phrases and topics associated with Russian propaganda narratives.
Expected outcomes
  • A structured and searchable database of Telegram messages relevant to the target audience.
  • Forming a set of themes for deeper analysis.
  • Empirical groundwork for the development of focus group discussion scenarios.
Step 2 of 4 Months 6–10

WP2:
Focus Group Implementation and Data Collection

Objective: To collect qualitative empirical data on the perception, interpretation, and emotional responses of Ukrainian refugees to disinformation-related themes disseminated through Telegram channels.

Key activities
  • Design and testing of focus group protocols based on findings from WP1.
  • Organisation and moderation of six online focus group sessions with participants from different German regions.
  • Audio/video recording and documentation of verbal and non-verbal reactions.
  • Supplementary field notes and reflexive memos recorded by the researcher.
Expected outcomes
  • A full set of focus group discussions.
  • Initial interpretations of how Ukrainian refugees engage with and respond to dominant disinformation themes.
  • Identification of alternative or unexpected interpretive patterns not evident in content analysis.
Step 3 of 4 Months 11–18

WP3:
Analysis and Synthesis of Focus Group Data

Objective: To conduct an inductive qualitative analysis of the focus group data, integrate findings with the preliminary content analysis, and formulate conclusions regarding the impact of propaganda narratives.

Key activities
  • Transcription of focus group recordings (using Whisper or similar AI engines).
  • Systematic coding and categorisation of themes and interpretive patterns.
  • Contextual interpretation of results, including attention to group dynamics and divergent reactions.
  • Integration and comparison with findings from WP1 to identify narrative effects and internalisation mechanisms.
Expected outcomes
  • A synthesised set of themes reflecting how disinformation is received and negotiated by refugees.
  • Evidence-based insights into the internalisation and resistance of propaganda narratives.
Step 4 of 4 Months 19–24

WP4:
Preparation and Publication of the Final Report

Objective: To consolidate the project results and present them in the form of a final report that integrates both academic findings and practical outputs.

Key activities
  • Systematizing and analyzing the results obtained in WP1 and WP3.
  • Preparing the final report that incorporates evidence-based conclusions and practical outcomes.
  • Developing an electronic layout of the report for publication and dissemination.
  • Disseminating the final report through partner networks, professional platforms, and other relevant channels.
Expected outcomes
  • Final report summarising the project results, relevant to policy, practice, and the academic community, and addressing the needs of the identified target groups.

Ethics and Data Security

Informed Consent

Detailed info provided before participation; voluntary involvement with the right to withdraw at any time.

Confidentiality (GDPR)

Data anonymised and stored on encrypted servers at the University of Mannheim with strict access control.

Psychological Safety

Interviewers trained in trauma-sensitive communication to avoid re-traumatisation of vulnerable refugees.

From Research to Action

Closing a critical gap in empirical research on the informational vulnerability of migrants in Western Europe.

Serving as a basis for practical recommendations for governmental and civil society organisations.

Integrating knowledge into European media literacy projects to enhance democratic resilience.
